## Intent (PM)
A user with a previously saved credit card should complete checkout in 3 clicks or fewer, without re-entering card details.

### Why this matters
- Reduces friction at the highest-value moment of the funnel.
- Conversion target: 65% checkout completion among saved-card users.
- Last incident: 2026-03 — saved-card flow broke after a Stripe API upgrade, affecting 24 hours of revenue.

### Out of scope
- New card entry (covered separately in `checkout-with-new-card`)
- 3DS authentication (covered separately in `checkout-with-3ds-card`)
- Subscription products (covered separately in `subscription-checkout`)

## Steps (AI exploration result)

### Preconditions
- Test user fixture: `users.with_saved_card` (Stripe customer ID `cus_test_xxx`, 1 saved card)
- Test items: `items.standard_10usd` ($10.00 USD)

### Flow
1. **Login** — Sign in as `users.with_saved_card` (uses `auth-flow` skill)
2. **Navigate** — Go to `/cart` (assert `await expect(page).toHaveURL("/cart")`)
3. **Add item** — Add `items.standard_10usd` to cart (`data-testid="add-to-cart-button"`)
4. **Verify cart total** — `data-testid="cart-total"` shows `$10.00`
5. **Click pay button** — `data-testid="checkout-pay-with-saved-card"`
6. **Verify modal amount** — `data-testid="checkout-confirm-amount"` shows `$10.00` (High Risk: amount mismatch can occur here)
7. **Confirm** — Click `data-testid="checkout-confirm-button"`
8. **Wait for Stripe webhook** — `await page.waitForURL(/\/orders\/[a-z0-9-]+$/)` (uses `payment-flow` skill)
9. **Verify order** — On the order page:
    - `data-testid="order-status"` === `"paid"`
    - `data-testid="order-amount"` === `"$10.00"`
    - `data-testid="order-items"` contains 1 entry of `items.standard_10usd`

### Click count
3 clicks (steps 5 and 7 are user clicks; step 3 is preparation and removable)
